Our Facilities

Food

Our comfortable and atmospheric restaurant provides the perfect setting for a relaxing meal. Our pirates provide hospitality that is legendary and the food it to DIE for. We serve a wide selection of dishes from the land and sea with locally caught seafood being our speciality. We are open for lunch and dinner on a daily basis and also serve a wide range of light bites and snacks in the bar.

Music

The Hollow Yacht provides live entertainment nightly. We showcase the extensive local talent who provide all our clientele with the best of ceol agus craic. Locals and visitors are welcome to join in and join the musicians in a song or tune. A fun and entertaining night is guaranteed for all. Leave your inhibitions at the door.

Bar

Oh ye Oh Ye, come in and rest your weary limbs in our fine establishment and have a libation ---or few. The Hollow Yacht incorporates a rustic feel with a warm and comfortable ambience where you can enjoy a wide selection of draught and bottled beers, fine wines and spirits and the odd bit of contraband. Our staff are always willing to relieve you of your pieces of gold and ensure you go merrily on your way.

History

The Hollow Yacht has a long and not always celebrated history.

  • 1794

    Setting Sail

    "The Hollow Yacht" was founded in 1794 by the Murphy brothers, built using the proceeds from years of smuggling up and down the West Coast of Ireland.

  • 1804

    Tragedy Strikes!

    Disaster hits when the worst storm in 100 years results in 4 Spanish ships being wrecked off our coast. These ships were carrying a bounty of goods that were washed ashore on the local beaches and provided a generous income for the local population for the next 10 years.

  • 1916

    Gun Running For The Revolution

    As part of the preparations for the Rising of 1916 the yacht Oceanica was used to smuggle guns and ammunition to supply the revolutionaries. Although there were a number of successful landings the British forces got wind of the activities and ambushed the Oceanica in March 1916 killing four of the crew and capturing the rest.

  • 1969

    Redevelopment

    The original building was re-modelled and extended to include a bar and restaurant. The new premises were officially opened by the local TD Joe Bloggs.

  • 1990

    New Owners

    The original family of Murphy's who had owned the premises for over 200 years decided it was time to move on and the bar and restaurant was acquired by local business man Mr. Moneybags.
